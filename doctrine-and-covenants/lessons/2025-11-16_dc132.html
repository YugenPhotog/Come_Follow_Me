Historical Reality
For the first 200-300 years after Christ, Christian teachers openly taught that humans could become like God. This wasn't considered heretical or strange‚Äîit was mainstream Christian doctrine.

What Changed?

The Doctrine of Creation Ex Nihilo

The Gospel Topics essay explains that changing views on Creation contributed to the loss of deification doctrine:

  1. Early teaching: God organized the world from preexisting materials
  2. 2nd century shift: Greek philosophy influenced theology, leading to creation ex nihilo‚Äî"out of nothing"
  3. Consequences: To emphasize God's unique power, theologians reasoned nothing could have existed as long as God had
  4. Widening gulf: Creation ex nihilo widened the perceived distance between God and humans

Result: The first known objection to deification came in the fifth century. By the sixth century, teachings were limited. Deification "faded from Western Christianity" though it "remains a central tenet of Eastern Orthodoxy."

Theological Connections

Theological Connections

  • If God created everything from nothing, He's fundamentally different from His creation in kind, not just degree
  • If humans were created from nothing, they can't have divine essence or eternal souls
  • If nothing existed before God's creative act, humans can't have pre-existed as His spirit children
  • The doctrine emphasizes God's absolute transcendence rather than His role as our Father
  • It makes humans "creatures" rather than "children"
