Image of God (Imago Dei)

The doctrine that humans are created to reflect God's nature‚Äîphysically (He has a glorified body) and spiritually (we have divine potential). In LDS theology, this is literal: we are spirit children of heavenly parents.

This is one of the most distinctive LDS doctrines. We believe God has a glorified, physical body and that we are literally created in His image‚Äînot merely metaphorically. This gives every human being inherent worth and divine potential. Ask: How should this affect how we treat others? How we see ourselves when we struggle?

Dominion (Hebrew: radah)

Often translated "rule" or "have dominion," but implies responsible stewardship rather than exploitation. D&C 121 teaches that dominion should be maintained by "persuasion, long-suffering, gentleness, meekness, and love unfeigned."

Elder G√©rald Causs√© spoke on "Our Earthly Stewardship" (Oct 2022).
