\## The Rectification of Names



\* In Confucianism, the concept of "rectification of names" (\*zhengming\*) holds that words should correspond to reality, and people should fulfill the true meaning of their roles and titles.





\* For example, a true ruler governs with wisdom, a true parent nurtures their children, and a true child respects their parents.





\* When names accurately reflect their essence, social harmony is maintained.





\* Confucius redefined the term "\*junzi\*"—which originally meant "son of a lord" by birth—to describe someone who embodies moral excellence, wisdom, and virtue, regardless of their birth status.





\* A true \*junzi\* cultivates virtues like benevolence and righteousness, acting as a moral exemplar in society.





\* Today, \*junzi\* is commonly translated as "gentleman," reflecting its ethical rather than aristocratic meaning.
